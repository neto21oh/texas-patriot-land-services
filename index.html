Business Fundability

Credit Building
Roadmap

A properly structured business credit profile unlocks equipment financing, government contracts, and commercial lending — critical for scaling from a single-tractor operation to a multi-crew fleet. Here is the step-by-step plan.

01

LLC Formation

File Texas LLC through the Secretary of State. Use the business name exactly as branded. Obtain a registered agent. Keep personal and business assets legally separated from day one.

Texas SOS $300 Filing Registered Agent
02

EIN Setup

Apply for an Employer Identification Number (EIN) through the IRS immediately after LLC approval. This is your business's Social Security Number — used for all banking, credit, and tax purposes.

IRS.gov (Free) Same Day Online Required for Banking
03

Business Banking

Open a dedicated business checking account under the LLC name and EIN. Use a local Texas bank or credit union when possible. Never comingle personal and business funds. Aim for 90 days of average daily balance before applying for credit.

Texas Bank / CU Separate Account 90-Day Season
04

DUNS / UEI Number

Register for a Unique Entity Identifier (UEI) through SAM.gov — this replaced the DUNS number for federal contracting. Required for all government work. Free registration, process takes 1–2 weeks.

SAM.gov (Free) Federal Contracts Annual Renewal
05

SAM.gov Registration

Complete full System for Award Management (SAM.gov) registration — required for all federal contracts, grants, and subcontracting. Include NAICS codes: 115310 (Support Activities for Forestry), 561730 (Landscaping), 238910 (Site Preparation).

NAICS 115310 NAICS 561730 SDVOSB Flag
06

Net-30 Vendor Accounts

Open 3–5 Net-30 trade accounts immediately. These report to Dun & Bradstreet and build your Paydex score. Start with: Uline, Grainger, HD Supply, Quill, Sunbelt Rentals. Pay early — always before the due date.

Uline Grainger Quill HD Supply Sunbelt
07

Vendor Tradelines

After 90 days of Net-30 activity, apply for a business credit card (Ramp, Brex, or Capital One Spark). Use for fuel, supplies, and equipment — never personal expenses. Keep utilization under 30% and pay in full monthly.

Ramp / Brex <30% Utilization Pay In Full
08

Equipment Financing

After 6+ months of business banking history and a Paydex score of 80+, approach equipment lenders. Farm Credit, AgDirect, and Kubota Credit offer favorable terms for agricultural equipment. Seller-financed used equipment is also a strong early option.

Farm Credit AgDirect Kubota Credit Paydex 80+
09

SDVOSB Certification

Apply for Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business (SDVOSB) or Veteran-Owned Small Business (VOSB) certification through the VA's SBA Vet Cert program. Opens set-aside federal contracts specifically designated for veteran-owned companies.

VA SBA VetCert Set-Aside Contracts SDVOSB / VOSB
10

Commercial Fundability

By month 18–24, you should have: D&B Paydex 80+, Experian Intelliscore 70+, 2 years tax returns, documented revenue, and a bankable P&L. This profile qualifies for SBA 7(a) loans, equipment lines of credit, and commercial real estate deposits.

SBA 7(a) Paydex 80+ Equipment LOC 2-Yr Tax Returns
